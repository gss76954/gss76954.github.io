Gibson J-45

The J-45 (also known as "the workhorse") is one of Gibson's best-selling acoustic guitars ever. It was first created in 1942 and has been used by numerous artists all over the world. The J-45 is known for its deep, rich tone and sounds better the harder you strum it.

This beautiful guitar is a round-shoulder dreadnought shape. It is composed of a sitka spruce top, with mohogany on the back and sides. The neck is also made of mahogany and features a rosewood fingerboard. The bridge is also made of rosewood and the nut and saddle are composed of tusq. The body uses an X Scalloped bracing method and features a dovetail neck joint. The J-45 also includes an LR Baggs VTC pickup system that sounds great when plugged in on stage.

Here are the full specs:

  • String Type: Steel
  • Number of Strings: 6
  • Body Shape: Round Shoulder Dreadnought
  • Finish: Nitrocellulose Lacquer
  • Top Wood: Sitka Spruce
  • Back and Sides Wood: Mahogany
  • Body Bracing: X Scalloped
  • Neck Wood: Mahogany
  • Fingerboard Material: Rosewood
  • Tuners: Nickel Grover Rotomatics
  • Bridge Material: Rosewood
  • Nut and Saddle Material: Tusq
  • Electronics: LR Baggs Element VTC

*A few notable artists that use the J-45 as their main guitar are Kacey Musgraves, Mike Rosenburg, Justin Moore, Shelby Lynne, Ryan Bingham, and Kristian Stanfill.
